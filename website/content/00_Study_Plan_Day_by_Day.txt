     For every concept, write actual code. Even if it's a small example.

     Interviewers assess communication as much as knowledge.

     Each week has [R] revision tasks for a reason.

  4. TRACK YOUR WEAK AREAS — Write them down. Revisit them.
     Don't avoid hard topics; spend extra time on them.

     Pressure-practice is the only way to get interview-ready.

  6. PRIORITIZE STARRED [*] TOPICS — If time is short, these
     come up most frequently in actual interviews.

  7. QUALITY OVER QUANTITY — Deeply understanding 50 problems
     beats superficially reading 200 solutions.

  8. CONSISTENCY > INTENSITY — 2 hrs daily beats 10 hrs on weekends.

  9. REST IS PART OF PREPARATION — Sleep 7-8 hours.
     Memory consolidation happens during sleep.
